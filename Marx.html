Overall intruduction

Hegel (1770–1831) was a German philosopher who significantly influenced Western philosophy, particularly through his ideas on dialectics, history, and absolute idealism.
His work emphasized the development of self-consciousness and the unfolding of reason throughout history.


Marx's major contributions include:


  • The Communist Manifesto: Co-authored with Friedrich Engels, it outlines the necessity of proletarian revolution, calling on the working class to unite,
    overthrow capitalism, and establish a socialist society.
  • Capital: A comprehensive analysis of capitalist economics, where he introduced the theory of surplus value,
    revealing the nature of capital accumulation and the exploitation of workers.
  • Historical Materialism: The theory that history is driven by material production methods, with class struggle as the engine of social development, predicting the eventual collapse of capitalism and the rise of communism.
