Overall intruduction

Hegel (1770–1831) was a German philosopher who significantly influenced Western philosophy, particularly through his ideas on dialectics, history, and absolute idealism.
His work emphasized the development of self-consciousness and the unfolding of reason throughout history.


Hegel's major contributions include:
  • Phenomenology of Spirit: Examines the development of human consciousness and self-awareness through a dialectical process, culminating in absolute knowledge.
  • Science of Logic: Explores the principles of logic and the nature of reality, introducing the dialectical method as a way of understanding contradictions.
  • Encyclopedia of the Philosophical Sciences: A comprehensive overview of his philosophical system, covering logic, nature, and spirit, articulating the progression of knowledge and reality.
  • Philosophy of History: Analyzes the unfolding of history as a rational process, asserting that history has a purpose and moves toward the realization of freedom.
