Overall intruduction

Aristotle (384–322 BCE) was a Greek philosopher who shaped Western thought with his work in logic, ethics, politics, and science.
A student of Plato, he focused on empirical observation and categorized knowledge.
His concept of the "golden mean" in ethics and his influential works like Metaphysics and Politics continue to impact philosophy and science.
He had made those great works below:

  • Metaphysics: Explores questions of existence, causality, and the nature of reality.
  • Nicomachean Ethics: Discusses what it means to live a good life and introduces the concept of the "golden mean," advocating for virtue as the balance between extremes.
  • Politics: Examines the nature of human communities, the best forms of government, and how societies should be organized for the common good.
  • Poetics: Analyzes literary theory, particularly tragedy and epic poetry.
